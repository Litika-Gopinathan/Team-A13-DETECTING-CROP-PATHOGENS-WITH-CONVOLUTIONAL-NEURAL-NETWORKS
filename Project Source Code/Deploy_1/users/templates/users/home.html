What is Plant Pathogens?

Plant pathogens are very similar to those that cause disease in humans and animals. Fungi, fungal-like organisms, bacteria, phytoplasmas, viruses, viroids, nematodes, and parasitic higher plants are all plant pathogens.

Disadvantages of Crop Pathogens

Plant Disease Classifications

Plant Diseases and Their Descriptions

Anthracnose

Anthracnose is a plant disease caused by various fungi that leads to dark, sunken lesions on leaves, stems, and fruits. It can cause significant damage to crops.

Read More

Apple Scab

Apple Scab is a fungal disease that causes dark, sunken lesions on apple leaves and fruit. It can lead to poor fruit quality and yield reduction.

Read More

Botrytis

Botrytis, or gray mold, affects a wide range of plants. It causes grayish-brown mold to form on flowers, fruits, and leaves, often leading to rot and decay.

Read More

Brown Rot

Brown Rot is a fungal disease that primarily affects stone fruits like peaches and cherries. It causes fruit to become mushy and brown, often leading to premature fruit drop.

Read More

Cedar Apple Rust

Cedar Apple Rust affects apples and junipers, causing orange, spongy growths on leaves and fruit. It can reduce fruit quality and yield.

Read More

Crown Gall

Crown Gall is a bacterial disease that causes galls or tumors to form on plant roots and stems. It can stunt growth and reduce plant vigor.

Read More

Downy Mildew

Downy Mildew is a fungal disease that affects leaves, causing yellow patches and a white, powdery growth. It can lead to reduced crop yields.

Read More

Fire Blight

Fire Blight is a bacterial disease that affects fruit trees, causing blackened, wilted leaves and branches. It can result in significant plant loss.

Read More

Leaf Spots

Leaf Spots are caused by various pathogens, resulting in small, dark spots on leaves. They can lead to premature leaf drop and reduced photosynthesis.

Read More

Mosaic Virus

Mosaic Virus causes mottled, mosaic-like patterns on plant leaves. It can stunt growth and reduce yield in infected plants.

Read More

Powdery Mildew

Powdery Mildew is a fungal disease that appears as a white, powdery substance on leaves and stems. It can cause leaf curling and reduced plant growth.

Read More

Verticillium

Verticillium is a soil-borne fungal disease that causes wilting and yellowing of plant leaves. It can lead to significant plant damage and yield loss.
